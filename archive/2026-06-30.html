"Sermon: What Will You Leave Behind?"

Series: Made for Relationships · Preacher: Dan Lian

TODAY'S FOCUS: Biblical Models of Legacy Investment

KEY SCRIPTURE

1 Timothy 6:18 — "Command them to do good, to be rich in good works, and to be generous and ready to share with others."

INSIGHT

The Bible is filled with men and women who understood legacy as the transfer of faith, not just resources. Moses invested in Joshua. Eli mentored Samuel. Priscilla and Aquila took Apollos aside and taught him more accurately. Paul told Timothy to find faithful people who would then train others (2 Timothy 2:2). The pattern is consistent: spiritual legacy is multiplicative, not additive. You don't just pass it down one generation — you equip people who equip people. 1 Timothy 6:18 frames good works as something we are to be "rich in" — this is not casual or occasional, but a deliberate, generous orientation of life. Dan's sermon picked up this thread: in a culture that hoards knowledge and connections, the Christian response is radical openness and generosity with what we have learned about following Jesus.

TODAY'S APPLICATION

Identify one biblical model of mentorship or legacy (such as Moses and Joshua, or Priscilla and Aquila with Apollos). Read their story in one sitting and ask: What did this person do intentionally that I could replicate? Then pick one concrete practice from their example and apply it this week.

CLOSING PRAYER

Father, thank You for the men and women in Scripture who took seriously the call to invest in the next generation. Give me a teachable spirit as I seek to emulate them. I want to be generous with what you have given me — not hoard it out of fear, but share it with faith. Guard my heart from comparison or jealousy when I see others being used by You. In Christ's name, Amen.
