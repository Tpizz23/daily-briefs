Sermon Review

"Sermon: What Will You Leave Behind?"

Series: Made for Relationships · Preacher: Dan Lian

TODAY'S FOCUS: Jesus: The Ultimate Legacy Giver

KEY SCRIPTURE

"I have manifested Your name to the people You gave Me out of the world." — John 17:6

INSIGHT

The most complete picture of legacy investment is Jesus Himself. In John 17, Jesus prays to the Father not about buildings, programs, or institutional longevity, but about the people the Father gave Him — the disciples, and by extension, all who would follow through their testimony. Jesus spent three years with these men, doing life together: eating, walking, arguing, watching Him heal and teach and pray. He left them not a book or a building, but Himself — and then sent the Spirit so they would never feel alone in the mission. The sermon's emphasis on 'investing relationally in the next generation' finds its ultimate model in the Incarnation itself: God with us, walking alongside flawed people, trusting them with the Gospel. Dan challenged listeners to ask whether their relational investments actually look like Jesus — listening before speaking, serving before instructing, staying present even when it's costly.

TODAY'S APPLICATION

Think of a relationship where you have been more sender than listener — eager to impart information rather than receive the other person's story. In your next interaction with someone younger in faith, practice one discipline of Jesus: ask a question you don't already know the answer to, and listen without correcting.

CLOSING PRAYER

Jesus, You are the perfect example of what it means to give Yourself away. You invested everything in flawed people who would carry Your name to the ends of the earth. I want to invest the same way — not with my agenda, but with Your heart. Fill me with Your Spirit so that my presence with others would point them to You. Amen.
