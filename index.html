Sermon Review

"Getting On the Same Page" · Made For Relationships · Dan Lian

TODAY'S FOCUS: The Disconnect Between Intentions and Reality

KEY SCRIPTURE

Proverbs 18:13: "He who answers a matter before he hears it, it is folly and shame to him."

INSIGHT

Most of us enter relationships with good intentions. We want great friendships, a lasting marriage, a strong family. But good intentions aren't enough. Pastor Dan Lian pointed out that many people drift into relationships without understanding what it takes to build something that lasts. The problem isn't bad motives: it's missing the skills and self-awareness to act on good motives. The disconnect between what we intend and what we communicate is the quiet fracture in most broken relationships.

TODAY'S APPLICATION

Ask yourself: In which relationship do I assume I'm on the same page with someone when I haven't actually checked? Write down one specific conversation you need to have this week to verify that assumption.

CLOSING PRAYER

God, I confess that I've often assumed understanding where none exists. I want to be a person who listens before I speak, who asks before I assume. Give me the humility to seek clarity in my closest relationships. Open my eyes to where I've been blind. Amen.
