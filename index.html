Sermon Review

"Sermon: What Will You Leave Behind?"

Series: Made for Relationships · Preacher: Dan Lian

TODAY'S FOCUS: The Weight of What We Leave

KEY SCRIPTURE

Romans 14:7–8 — "None of us lives for ourselves, and none of us dies for ourselves. If we live, we live for the Lord, and if we die, we die for the Lord."

INSIGHT

Pastor Dan Lian opened week five of Made for Relationships by asking a question many of us avoid: What will you actually leave behind? In a cultural moment obsessed with building empires and accumulating assets, this sermon reframes legacy as relational investment — not financial inheritance. Romans 14:7–8 reminds us that our lives are not our own; they belong to the Lord. This means the most significant thing we can leave is not a thing at all, but a person who has been shaped, discipled, and loved because of our investment in them. The sermon pushback against the comfortable lie that we can delay this kind of investment: "I'll get serious about mentoring someone when I'm older, when I have more time." Dan's challenge is that the next generation needs us now, not later.

TODAY'S APPLICATION

Ask yourself: Who is one person younger than you that you are currently investing in — or could begin investing in? Schedule a conversation with them this week, not to teach, but to listen and learn who they are. Start with presence, not programs.

CLOSING PRAYER

Lord, I confess I have often thought about legacy in terms of what I can build or earn, rather than who I can pour into. Help me see the people you've placed around me as the real inheritance I leave. Give me eyes for those who need a faithful presence, and courage to start investing before I feel ready. In Jesus' name, Amen.
